DeCentering

2. Aesthetics, Appearance, Performance

Understanding the costs of aesthetic labor and performances needed to exist under the acceptable sphere of aesthetics, while facing fetishization and appropriation.

Themes:

Some definitions regarding aesthetics

How does thinking about aesthetics as a system of human interaction affect its meaning to people's lives? What gains power when the defintion of aesthetics becomes more than just an artisitc sense?

Safety and policing of the body

From discrimination based on body size, race, inferred class, disbaility, and more -- the body is policed in both digital and physical space. How does this affect our relationship with our bodies and others? Where do these relations draw from? How does this cause and punish labor?

Pretty privilege

To what extent has White mainstream culture been able to instill privilege in different spheres of life when someone conforms to the beauty standards?

Commodification, Fetishization, and Distaste

These terms are all things that occur to intersectionally marginalized bodies and aesthetics. And they occur on a spectrum, with implicit and explicit codes and conditions. In what ways do these judgements of aesthetics
