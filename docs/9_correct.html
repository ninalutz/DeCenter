DeCentering

9. Wrap

We covered the following sections:

Date Topic Summary
6/28 Social Media and Art Examining the ecosystem of trauma, content, and policing and its effects in marginalized lives on and offline.
6/5 and 7/12 Aesthetics, Appearance, Performance Understanding the costs of aesthetic labor and performances needed to exist under the acceptable sphere of aesthetics, while facing fetishization and appropriation.
7/19 Transportation and Movement Lack of autonomy and safety through movement of space and time. The labor of navigating rural, suburban, and urban spaces as a person with a marginalized body whilst under the eye of kyriarchy and white violence.
7/26 Careers and Professional Lives Professions uphold white supremacy culture and hierarchies -- causing abuse and ostracization of intersectionally marginalized workers. These social and professional costs of navigating workplaces, career arcs, and abuse can upend careers and lives.
8/2 Family and Reproductive Justice “Reproductive Justice”, introduced by SisterSong, states: “the human right to maintain personal bodily autonomy, have children, not have children, and parent the children we have in safe and sustainable communities”. This has been co-opted and abused by politics, adoption, and childcare complexes under racialized capitalism.
8/9 Health and Medical Space Navigating a racist, fatphobic, and sexist system with a marginalized body that is facing the effects of weathering from generational and present traumas.
8/16 Mental and Emotional Health A variety of systemic obstacles prevents the mental health betterment of individuals as they navigate racialized capitalism and traumas with the labor of performance.
8/23 Relationships, Sexuality Understanding that capitalism and cishet patriarchy systematically oppress a variety of relationships and the ability, value, and treatment of intersectionally marginalized individuals in their relationships and sexualities.
